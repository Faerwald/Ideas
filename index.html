Impossibilia amori cedunt.

“It’s not fiction.” — said by someone speaking words they didn’t fully understand, as if another mind were “behind/beyond” their eyes. He referred to an internet — not this one — and spoke with uncanny certainty about the name of the first person to travel to Mars, eerily aligned with my work. He said you could travel here from there. I kept an open mind and asked questions. He mentioned “National Counterspace” and “NCX.” Something strange happened earlier today; I don’t think he meant travel from some far island space beyond Trinidad and Tobago. He said “Albert” before it shifted to “Alfred.” My current guess: access is asymmetric — our 21st-century internet can’t reach them, but their network can thread into ours. For now, I’m going to rest my imagination; I have a headache.

Jason Agamemnon Sokaris
