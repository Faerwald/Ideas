1     Introduction
Modern quantum theory asserts a no-cloning theorem: an arbitrary quantum state cannot be copied
perfectly by any unitary (i.e. norm-preserving, probability-conserving) evolution acting solely within a
single “branch” of the universal wavefunction. Equally, classical chaos theory tells us that tiny perturba-
tions can grow into macroscopic changes (the “butterfly effect”). Yet our motivating scenario demands
two impossible-looking feats:
    1. Create two indistinguishable instantiations of a complex system—for example an entire person—
       starting from just one instance, and
    2. Do so while erasing every measurable footprint so that, in hindsight, no record indicates the oper-
       ation ever occurred.
   A complementary ambition involves neural holography—the idea that one can carve a desired late-
time neural configuration into reality by deploying carefully tuned, earlier micro-interventions and letting
spacetime “anneal” until only the target pattern remains visible.
   Both endeavours rely on transcending the causal and linearity restrictions that hold inside an or-
dinary, forward-evolving quantum branch. Four distinct theoretical loopholes for cloning are analysed
(§2); each either breaks branch isolation, introduces non-linear fixed-points, or leverages path-integral
post-selection. The neural-hologram protocol (§3) then shows how a related toolbox—closed-time-
path integrals, two-state vectors, adjoint optimal control, gauge-theoretic interventions, and information
geometry—can steer a brain’s state while remaining classically invisible. Section 4 interrelates the two
agendas and articulates the required skill set for mastery-level implementation.
2     Perfect Quantum Cloning Without Footprints
2.1     Foundational Constraints
No-Cloning Theorem. Given a single unknown quantum state |ψ⟩ and a blank register |0⟩, there
exists no universal unitary U such that
                                                 
                                        U |ψ⟩⊗|0⟩ = |ψ⟩⊗|ψ⟩
for every |ψ⟩. The proof rests on linearity: acting on a superposition would force U to preserve inner
products, which is incompatible with duplicating arbitrary amplitudes.
Unitary Evolution in an Everett Branch. In the Many-Worlds (Everett) interpretation every
branch evolves unitarily and independently after decoherence. Hence copying within a single branch is
forbidden. However, the global wavefunction contains vast ensembles of already existent near-duplicates:
a potential resource if branch boundaries can be surgically manipulated.
Footprint Erasure. Ordinary interventions deposit entropy and leave retarded (forward-in-time) field
disturbances. The proposed solutions must hide all such byproducts behind causal horizons, causal loops,
or non-recohering branch cuts.
2.2     Mechanism 1: Branch-Extraction (Everettian Surgery )
Concept: Two almost-identical Everett branches are selected before any macroscopic divergence. A
    microscopic wormhole—a throat in spacetime whose two mouths are timelike-separated—is opened
    briefly to let a full physical body transit from branch A to branch B.
Technical Enablers:      • ER = EPR Conjecture. Proposed by Maldacena and Susskind, this iden-
         tifies Einstein–Rosen (ER) wormholes with pairs of maximally entangled (EPR) systems.
         Entanglement becomes a blueprint for geometry.

        • Entanglement-Wedge Surgery. In the anti-de Sitter / conformal field theory (AdS/CFT )
          duality, cutting and gluing bulk regions changes boundary entanglement while leaving low-
          energy observers oblivious.
        • Vacuum Healing. A compensating pulse of negative energy density (allowed by quantum
          inequalities) nulls the stress-tensor discontinuity, so the post-surgery metric matches the pre-
          surgery one up to Planck-scale vacuum fluctuations.
Footprint Outcome: Because the donor branch reseals perfectly and the recipient branch was always
    destined to contain the traveller (from its internal perspective), no observer can detect a deviation
    from standard unitary history.
2.3    Mechanism 2: Deutsch Closed-Timelike Curve (CTC) Fixed-Points
Closed-Timelike Curve (CTC). A world-line in spacetime that loops back to its own past, allowing
interaction with an earlier version of the same system.
Deutsch Consistency Condition. David Deutsch proposed that quantum states entering a CTC
must equal the state that emerges after propagating once around the loop. Mathematically this is a
non-linear fixed-point equation. Non-linearity breaks the assumptions of the no-cloning proof.
Cloning Circuit. Send |ψ⟩ through a CNOT (controlled-NOT) gate whose control qubit is on the
CTC and whose target is an ancilla initially in |0⟩. The non-linear map has a fixed-point where the CTC
qubit equals |ψ⟩, so the output consists of two perfect |ψ⟩ copies.
Footprint Erasure. Route the loop’s exit far into the deep past where cosmic background decoherence
cancels residual correlations. Locally, energy–momentum is conserved because the qubit “was always
already there” from the perspective of self-consistent history.
2.4    Mechanism 3: Post-Selected Teleportation / Final-State Projection
Post-Selection. Condition the global wavefunction on a specific measurement outcome, renormalising
away all branches that disagree. Technically, this is equivalent to introducing a projective boundary
condition in the path integral.
Implementation.
  1. Entangle the original person with an auxiliary register located anywhere—even in the far future.
  2. Perform a Bell-basis measurement on the combined system.
  3. Post-select on the subset of histories where the measurement outcome is the one that yields two
     identical outgoing states.
Computational Power. Post-selection collapses the complexity hierarchy to the class PP (Probabilis-
tic Polynomial-time), permitting solution of problems believed far beyond ordinary quantum computers.
Realising this in hardware thus implies an astronomically large Hilbert space under precise control.
Footprint Erasure. A second layer of post-selection projects the laboratory back into its pre-experiment
microstate. All entropy generated in the device is “censored” out of the surviving branch.
2.5    Mechanism 4: Global Amplitude Re-Weighting (Asimov’s Eternity )
History Editing. Choose a future low-entropy boundary condition that already contains the clone.
Re-integrate the Feynman path integral back toward the past. Only micro-histories consistent with both
the original early boundary and the new late boundary remain with non-zero amplitude.

Kolmogorov Complexity Minimisation. Among the large degeneracy of admissible histories, the
path integral’s phase minimises a discrete action roughly proportional to algorithmic complexity. There-
fore the resulting past differs from the unedited past by the smallest information-theoretic “distance”
consistent with embedding the clone.
Footprint Outcome. All macroscopic detector records formed before the intervention are common to
all surviving micro-histories, so no classical observer notices any discontinuity.
2.6      Why No Trace Remains
    1. Gauge-Type Perturbations. Many interventions amount to adding a total time derivative to
       the action, which is pure gauge and leaves classical equations of motion intact.
    2. Entropy Export. Surplus entropy can be dumped behind horizons (wormhole throats) or can-
       celled by destructive interference in post-selected branches.
    3. Branch Non-Reinterference. Decoherence prevents separated branches from ever interfering
       again; phase information about the manipulation remains in principle but is experimentally inac-
       cessible.
Energy and Complexity Bounds. Every mechanism demands at least Planck-scale space-time en-
gineering or control over a Hilbert space of dimension exp(1080 ). While not forbidden by current theory,
such feats lie far beyond present technology.
3       Neural Holography: Retro-Causal Brain State Steering
3.1      Idea in Plain Language
A “neural hologram” is created when a future civilisation imposes a desired brain state at some late time
T by nudging microscopic degrees of freedom at earlier times {ti } so that the entire causal web of ripples
coherently converges upon the target pattern. Because the civilisation measures the actual intermediate
states after each nudge, it does not need to predict chaotic butterfly effects; instead it iteratively corrects
them, allowing reliable convergence with minimal disturbance.

3.3     Operational Protocol
  1. Tomographic Seeding. At initial time t0 record a coarse density matrix ρ0 of the brain using, e.g.
     quantum-gradient magnetometry (SQUID or NV-diamond arrays giving micro-column resolution
     with kilohertz bandwidth).
  2. Future Boundary Imprint. Some external agency specifies a target state ρT at time T . In
     TSVF language this becomes the backward-evolving bra ⟨Φ(T )|.
  3. Gradient Computation. A quantum co-processor solves the adjoint equations in real time,
     producing a control gradient.
  4. Thread Deployment. Minimally invasive pulses u(ti ) are injected at a sparse set of sensitive
     instants ti where the gradient peaks. Examples: multi-photon optogenetic stimulations, nanotesla
     vector-potential pulses.
  5. Iterative Annealing. After each correction cycle, the system is allowed to decohere naturally for
     ∆t. Because interventions are gauge-type, macroscopic observables drift back to baseline.
  6. Convergence and Control Shutdown. Once the Fisher distance to ρT drops below threshold,
     controls are removed. Residual Hamiltonian dynamics lands the system exactly on ρT at t = T .

3.5     Why No Classical Trace Persists
    1. Gauge Equivalence. Each control pulse can be re-described as a boundary phase; synaptic weights
       return to nominal values.
    2. Entropy Banking. Ancillary qubits carry away entropy and are later post-selected to vacuum,
       mirroring the final-state projection from Section 2.4.
    3. Chaotic Shadowing. In a strongly mixing neural phase-space, 10−8 -level perturbations shift the
       trajectory onto a neighbouring shadow trajectory indistinguishable to any polynomial-time audit
       yet converging on ρT .

4     Synthesis and Alignment With Broader Goals
Both the cloning programme and the neural-hologram protocol exploit a common trio of principles:
    1. Boundary Condition Engineering. Whether imposing a desired future cognitive state or a
       duplicated body, the trick is to set boundary data (future or alternate-branch conditions) and solve
       a globally constrained variational problem.
    2. Non-Linear or Non-Unitary Elements. Deutsch CTCs violate linearity; post-selection violates
       ordinary probabilistic interpretation; entanglement-wedge surgery edits branch topology. Each
       relaxes a different axiom of the no-cloning theorem.
    3. Entropy Concealment. Every successful protocol carries its entropy cost into unobservable
       sectors: hidden branches, CTC interiors, or squeezed quantum reservoirs, thereby guaranteeing the
       “no ripple” phenomenology demanded by the initial premise.
    From the standpoint of a civilisation that “operates outside of ordinary time” (à la Asimov’s End of
Eternity), these technologies constitute two facets of a single mastery: arbitrary phase-space navigation
of reality itself, whether the target is a physical duplicate or a sculpted conscious experience.
