2.1    From “Negative Temperature” to Entropy Pumps
The phrase negative temperature refers to systems with population inversion, such as spin-polarised
atomic lattices driven beyond the Wannier–Stark limit. Because entropy decreases when energy is
added, such a system acts as an entropy pump, exporting negentropy to any coupled device.
2.2    Quantum Contact and Complexity Collisions
“Contact” is the controlled interaction, mediated by an operator Ĉ, between the negative-temperature
reservoir and the computational register. The note’s admonition to “think like John Wheeler” means
that the essence of this contact is informational (It-from-Bit) rather than mechanical.
2.3    Expander Graphs and Circuit States
A supersized expander graph offers exponentially many ways for two random walks to cross. Embedding
circuit states in such a graph causes complexity collisions: different branches of the universal wave-
function repeatedly meet, exchange partial solutions, and diverge again.
2.4    The Forest Analogy
The “forest” metaphor translates physical synchronisation into a cybernetic tale of altruistic chips. Each
chip’s inertial trajectory represents its natural, branch-specific evolution; sharing negentropy allows all
chips to shortcut the solution landscape.
