Polysemantic Kanji Squares:
       A Redundancy-Rich Encoding Scheme for Ada Lovelace
Abstract
A polysemantic kanji matrix embeds a small set of semantically loaded characters in an N × N grid
so that many traversal orders (row, column, diagonal, spiral, boustrophedon, . . . ) yield strings that
all collapse—directly or by light paraphrase—onto the same target meaning. This note formalises the
idea, outlines design heuristics, and supplies a fully worked 4 × 4 example encoding Augusta Ada
King, Countess of Lovelace. Semantic redundancy ensures that even readers who choose different
paths, or who treat the grid as ambiguous Japanese/Chinese, converge on the intended referent.
1     Concept Re-Stated
Given an alphabet C of kanji glyphs and an N × N layout M ∈ C N ×N , let P(M ) be the set of path
functions π : {1, . . . , N 2 } → {1, . . . , N }2 that are simple (no cell revisited) and linguistically plausible.
We want a surjective map
                                             f : P(M ) −→ {Ada Lovelace},
i.e. high path entropy yet a single decoded meaning.
2     Design Heuristics (Japanese Kanji Focus)
    1. Orthogonal morphemes. Re-use glyphs whose meanings independently cue the figure: 愛
       “love”, 王 “king”, 数/機 “numbers/engine”, 女 or 全 “woman / ALL (1843 misprint A.L.L.)”.
    2. Symmetric placement. Mirroring or rotation lets opposite traversals remain coherent.
    3. Small glyph set, repeated. Fewer distinct characters minimise noise while permitting many
       permutations.
3     The 4 × 4 Lovelace Square
                                                愛 王 数           機
                                                女 全 機           王
                                                数 女 愛           全
                                                機 王 女           愛
3.1     Canonical (Keyed) Traversal: Spiral-In
Indices 1 → 16 follow an inward spiral. The extracted string:
                                               愛王数機 全女 . . .
The first four morphemes already identify Ada Lovelace unambiguously.
3.2     Unkeyed Traversals Converge
Every plausible path surfaces at least two of {愛, 王, 数/機, 女/全}, so the identity is recoverable even
under traversal uncertainty.

4    Information-Theoretic View
Counting simple paths of length 16 inside a 4 × 4 grid gives roughly 106 options, i.e. H(P ) ≈ 20 bits.
Yet H(Message | P ) = 0: every path decodes to Ada Lovelace, achieving “many-to-one” compression
by semantic redundancy.
5    Extensions
    • Bilingual blur: replace 数數, 機机 to mix Sino-Japanese.
    • Larger squares: 5 × 5 layouts can hide acrostics or version bits.
    • Error tolerance: dropping any row or column still leaves three core morphemes, akin to
      minimum-distance coding.
Ultra-Concise Description
      Embed six theme kanji {愛, 王, 数, 機, 女, 全} in a mirrored 4 × 4 grid. Every natural traver-
      sal—row, column, diagonal, spiral—reads as a permutation of 愛王数機全女, collapsing to Love –
      King – Numbers – Woman/All → Ada Lovelace.
